Sample Visitation Data Analysis - Three Chembur Branches, Mumbai

Executive Summary

This comparative analysis examines customer profile variations across three Yes Bank branches in Chembur, Mumbai, based on sample visitation data comprising 8,859 net footfall observations after accounting for turn-in. The data reveals distinct customer demographic patterns, with Laxmi Narayan Building recording 4,540 visitors, Shrikant Chambers with 2,963 visitors, and Villa Geraldine with 1,356 visitors. Each branch demonstrates markedly different customer segments in terms of household income distribution, lifestyle segmentation patterns, and financial product preferences. These variations provide valuable insights into the diverse customer bases served by each location, with average household incomes ranging from ₹119,100 to ₹134,500 per month across the branches.

Key Comparative Insights

Income Profile Distinctions

Villa Geraldine demonstrates a unique income concentration with 25.1% of visitors in the ₹2.3L-3L bracket, five times higher than Laxmi Narayan's 5.4%. The average household income at Villa Geraldine reaches ₹134,500, compared to ₹119,100 at Laxmi Narayan, reflecting distinctly different economic segments served by each branch.

Lifestyle Segmentation Variance

Urban New Wealth represents 28.4% of Villa Geraldine's visitors versus only 13.1% at Laxmi Narayan, while Conservative Urban Middle Class dominates at Laxmi Narayan (22.1%) compared to Villa Geraldine (15.3%). This indicates fundamentally different customer personas across locations.

Product Affinity Variations

ULIP product interest at Villa Geraldine (33.3%) is more than double that of Laxmi Narayan (15.3%), while retirement plan interest shows the opposite pattern with Laxmi Narayan at 53.7% versus Villa Geraldine at 39.7%, suggesting different life stage and investment preferences.

Elite Segment Distribution

Urban Established Elite representation drops by half at Villa Geraldine (8.2%) compared to the other branches (16-17%), despite Villa Geraldine having the highest average income, indicating that wealth alone doesn't determine lifestyle segmentation patterns.

Credit Usage Expectations

Expected heavy credit card usage increases progressively from Laxmi Narayan (43.9%) to Villa Geraldine (50.1%), correlating with the higher concentration of Urban New Wealth segments and higher average household incomes.

Customer Base Characteristics

Each branch serves fundamentally different customer profiles despite geographic proximity. The variations in income distribution, lifestyle segments, and product preferences suggest that branch-specific strategies may be more effective than uniform approaches across the network.
